Who We Are

RAC (Real Autism Change) is a passionate and dedicated organization committed to making a positive impact in the lives of individuals and families affected by autism every day. Our motto, "Creating a Change, Every Day, for Every Family," reflects our mission to provide vital support and resources that empower individuals with autism and their families. The organization focuses on offering respite care services in Ablekuman, connecting families with autism to specialists and educators, and advocating for autism-related legislation. RAD was founded by Oduro Solomon, driven by their personal experience with a younger sister who has severe Autism Spectrum Disorder. This experience ignited a deep commitment to advocating for individuals with autism and ensuring they have access to the resources and care they need to thrive.

Solomon's time at RAC (Real Autism Change) further highlighted the challenges faced by individuals with autism and their families in accessing quality care and resources, especially in navigating daily life and education. At RAC, we believe that every individual with autism deserves equal opportunities for support and inclusion. We work tirelessly to address these barriers by connecting families with specialists, educators, and respite care services tailored to their specific needs. Additionally, we actively advocate for autism legislation, ensuring that individuals with autism have the necessary resources to thrive. With a deep passion for helping families and children with autism, RAC is dedicated to making a lasting difference in the lives of those we serve. We believe in inclusive care, community education, and advocacy to improve access to resources and the overall quality of life for individuals with autism. If you would like to learn more about RAC or get involved with our initiatives, please do not hesitate to reach out. We eagerly look forward to hearing from you and working together toward our shared goal of making a positive impact in the autism community every day.

Strategies and Recommendations for Educators to Positively Impact the Lives of Individuals and Families with Autism

Personalized Learning Plans

Each child with autism has unique strengths and challenges. Educators should collaborate with parents, specialists, and therapists to develop individualized education plans (IEPs) tailored to each student's needs. Personalized learning strategies ensure that every child can succeed at their own pace.

A Structured and Predictable Classroom Environment

Consistency is key for children with autism. Educators should develop predictable routines, clearly communicate changes, and provide structure in daily activities. This allows students with autism to feel safe and reduces anxiety, fostering a learning environment where they can thrive.

Visual Supports and Clear Instructions

Visual supports, such as picture schedules, visual task cards, or step-by-step diagrams, can help students with autism understand and follow instructions more easily. Educators should combine these with verbal instructions to cater to different learning styles.

Sensory-Friendly Strategies

Many students with autism experience sensory sensitivities. Educators can create a sensory-friendly classroom by minimizing harsh lighting, loud noises, and strong smells. Offering sensory breaks or providing calming tools such as fidget toys or noise-canceling headphones can help students manage overstimulation and remain focused.

Social Skills and Emotional Learning

Social interactions and understanding emotions can be challenging for children with autism. Educators should provide opportunities for social-emotional learning through role-playing, social stories, and structured group activities.

Peer Support and Inclusion

Inclusive classrooms where all students work together foster a sense of belonging for children with autism. Educators can encourage peer support by assigning buddies, promoting group activities, and teaching students about neurodiversity.

Positive Behavior Support

Behavioral challenges are common among children with autism, and educators can use positive behavior strategies to address them. Reinforcing positive behaviors, providing clear expectations, and offering rewards for achievements can create a motivating and supportive classroom environment.

Maintain Open Communication with Families

Building a strong partnership with families is essential for supporting children with autism. Educators should maintain open, regular communication with parents, caregivers, and other professionals involved in the child's life.
